Ginger Pork Noodles Stir-Fry

Description

A delicious stir-fry mix of Udon Noodles, Pak Choy, Carrot, Pork and Gingery goodness that is simple and quick to make and highly customisable. Minimal prep work and cleanup, perfect for those nights when you just want something quick, easy and tasty!

Suitable for beginners or experienced chefs alike, you can also tailor this dish to your own preferences and make alterations freely!

Ingredients

  • Olive Oil
  • Carrots : 2
  • Pak Choy : 2 Bunches
  • Garlic : 4 Cloves
  • Udon Noodles : 2 Packets
  • Oyster Sauce : 100g
  • Brown Sugar : 2 Teaspoons
  • White Whine Vinegar : 1 Tablespoon
  • Soy Sauce : 2 Tablespoons
  • Pork Mince : 500g
  • Ginger Paste : 30g
  • Butter : 40g


Steps

  1. Boil a full kettle, thinly slice carrots into half-moons and roughly chop Pak Choy.
  2. Finely chop or crush garlic.
  3. In a large pan, heat a drizzle of olive oil over high heat and cook carrot until tender, 4-5 mins.
  4. Add garlc and Pak Choy and cook until fragrant, 1-2 mins. Transfer to a bowl.
  5. Half-fill a medium saucepan with boiling water, and add the Udon Noodles to it.
  6. Cook the noodles over a medium-high heat until tender, 3-4 mins.
  7. In the last minute of cook time, gently stir the noodles with a fork to separate. Drain, rinse and set aside.
  8. Meanwhile, combine the oyster sauce, brown sugar, vinegar, soy sauce and a splash of water in a small bowl.
  9. Return the large pan to high heat with a drizzle of olive oil. Once hot, cook the pork mince, breaking it up with a spoon, 3-4 mins.
  10. Add the ginger paste and cook until fragrant, 1 min.
  11. Add the oyster sauce mixture, butter, cooked noodles and vegetables to the pan.
  12. Stir-fry to combine and complete the recipe, season with salt and pepper.
