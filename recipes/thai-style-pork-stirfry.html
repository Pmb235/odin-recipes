Thai-Style Pork Stir-Fry

Description

A delicious thai-style pork stirfry with veggies and mouth-watering buttered garlic rice. A future family favourite: quick, clean and easy to make.

Suitable for beginners in the kitchen and vegetarian option if you switch the pork to tofu!

Ingredients

  • Olive Oil
  • Garlic : 4 Cloves
  • Butter : 40g
  • Basmati Rice : 2 cups
  • Water : 3 cups
  • Carrot : 2
  • Green Beans : 400g
  • Oyster Sauce : 120g
  • Sweet Chili Sauce : 120g
  • White Wine Vinegar : 1 TBSP
  • Water(For the sauce) : 2 TBSP
  • Pork Mince : 500g
  • Crunched Peanuts : 100g

Steps

  1. Finely chop or crush garlic. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter with a dahs of olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Cook garlic until fragrant, 1-2 mins.
  3. Add basmati rice, water(for the rice) and a generous pinch of salt and bring to the boil. Reduce heat to low and cover with a lid.
  4. Cook for 10 mins, and then remove from heat and keep covered until rice is tender and water absorbed, 10 mins.
  5. Meanwhile, thinly slice carrots into half-moons. Trim and halve green beans.
  6. In a small bowl, combine oyster sauce, sweet chili sauce, a drizzle of white wine vinegar and the water (for the sauce).
  7. In a large frying pan, heat a drizzle of olive oil over medium-high heat.
  8. Stir-fry carrot and beans until tender, 4-5 mins. Transfer to a plate.
  9. Return frying pan to high heat with a drizzle of oil. Cook pork mince until browned, 2-3 mins.
  10. Retyrb veggies to pan and add sauce mixture. Stir-fry until slightly reduced, 1-2 mins.
  11. Stir crushed peanuts through the pork and veggies.
  12. Divide garlic rice between plates and top up with thai-style pork stir-fry mix.
